Military Park: Significance through History

Military Park is Founded

The founders of Newark set aside a six-acre stretch that formed a triangle between Broad Street, Park Place, and Rector Street. It was initially referrd to as the "Middle Commons." In the 1700's it was referred to as the "Training Grounds" used for military training. And finally, "Military Park" from 1869 onwards.

1666

First Major Military Use

Men were called to Military Park, then referred to as the "Training Grounds," to respond to Native American unrest throughout New England.

September 6, 1673

Officially Deemed Drill Ground

A town meeting was held, and it was agreed upon that Military Park would be used for militial training. "Another triangle piece. Allotted for training place, in length on the west side 17 chaines, on the southeast side 15 chaines. And at the East End 7 chaines and a halfe bounded on all sides by highways."

1674

Newark's Second Oldest Church, Trinity & St. Philips Episcopal Church, Established in Northern End of Military Park

In 1733, Colonel Josiah Ogden was reprimanded by the First Church (a Presbyterian church) for breaking Sabbath by harvesting his almost spoilt wheat crop on a Sunday. Since the town was deeply religious, Ogden was outcast from the community and unhappy with the congregation of the First Church. Along with a small group of Episcopalians, Ogden established the Trinity Episcopal Church, Newark's second oldest church. The bitter feud between the First Church and the Second Church lasted until the Revolutionary War. George Washington was said to have attended services at this church as well.

George Washington Marches Through

Newark citizens gathered in Military Park to watch Washington march through on his way north to Massachusetts to lead the Continental Army.

June 1775

George Washington and Troops Retreat from Fort Lee

Washing and his Troops were noted to have camped out in Military Park while the retreating during the Revolutionary War. It is also rumoured that Thomas Paine began writing his famous essay The American Crisis at this time

June 1775

"Training Place" Converted to a Municipal Park

A town meeting was held, and it was agreed upon that the drill grounds would be converted to a park. Elm trees were planted around the perimeter of the park.

1813

More Renovations

The town put more work into the park's aesthetics. Iron fence and some landscaping done in the park's interior. Watering troughs installed to appeal to townspeople as a gathering place while they let their horses graze.

1838

First Protest!

Following the economic downturn of the Panic of 1857, more than 2,000 workers marched in Military Park demanding unemployment relief.

1857

Founding Member of NJIT Edward Weston Provides Electricity for Military Park

Weston was a master electrician who's main focus was with dynamos (an electrical generator that produces direct current with the use of a commutator). He worked through the 1870's on maximizing efficiency of the dynamos and debuted his dynamo powered electric lamps in 1876. He moved to Newark in 1877 and established himself by putting up his lamps on the watchtower of Newark's Fire Department which was in the town center. The publicity from those lamps led to the city of Newark ordering his lamps for Military Park, which was also the first municipal underwriting of electrical street lighting in the United States. Weston also later went on to light the Brooklyn Bridge.

Read more 1878

Statue of General Philip Kearny Erected in Military Park

The statue of the one-armed Civil War hero and Newark native was the first public sculpture placed in Newark. The statue was initially commissioned in 1873 by the State of New Jersey to be put on display in Statuary Hall located in Washington D.C.. However, the statue was found in the cellar of the State House in Trenton in 1879. Once found, a Civil War veteran committee arranged to move the statue to Newark. Ulysses S. Grant and George B. McClellan attended the dedication ceremony. Henry Kirke, also famous for the casting of the George Washington statue in Union Square casted the bronze statue.

Spanish Cannon from Spanish-American War presented to Newark

The cannon, known as the "Spanish Cannon," used in the Smpanish American War originated from the Morrow Castle Fortress in Santiago Harbor, Puerto Rico

July 4, 1899

Statue of Frederick T. Frelinghuysen Commissioned

The Board of Trade organized to have a bronze statue of Frederick T. Frelinghuysen installed at Military Park. Frelinghuysen dedicated his life to serving New Jersey as an attorney general, United States senator, and Secretary of State to President Chester A. Arther. Karl Gerhardt sculpted the statue.

1904

Howitzers Used in Civil War Installed

Two howitzers from Commodore Perry's 1853 flagship to the Orient were installed in Military Park.

1906

"Wars of America" Monument Takes Over Military Park

Civil War veteran and Newark merchant Amos Van Horn's estate commissioned Gutzon Borglum (also famous for sculpting Mount Rushmore) to memorialize the major wars America had participated in up until World War I. Van Horn bequeathed $100,000 to the cause. This monument took Borglum six years to complete and it is dedicated to all the American citizens that had dedicated their lives for America's freedom. This monument features fourty two humans and two horses and represents a character from each of the different wars (Revolutionary War, Civil War, Spanish-American War, and World War I). Among the great leaders of these wars are some other famous figures in this monument, such as John Purroy Mitchel (second youngest mayor of New York and aviator in World War I) and Amos Van Horn, the man who commissioned the monument.

1926

Underground Parking Garage Added

Due to the increase of commuters in Newark, the city sought relief by constructing a multi-level parking garage capable of holding up to 1,030 cars. The construction required entrance and exit ramps, along with stair and elevator kisoks which interfered the natural landscape and location of the monuments. Due to this disruption, certain statues were repositioned to accommodate the new landscape.

1960

John F. Kennedy Sculpture Unveiled

The bust of John F. Kennedy was commissioned by a committee on behalf of Essex County. This was the last monument to be installed in Military Park. Jacques Lipschitz sculpted the bust, and the Grad firm (noted architectural firm consisting of Frank Grad and his two sons) designed the pedestal for the bust.

1965

President Lyndon Johnson attended Democratic Rally at Military Park

Democratic Rally held at Military Park. President Johnson stops by to give a speech.

October 7, 1966

Riots breakout through Newark due to civil unrest following repeated violation of basic human rights
