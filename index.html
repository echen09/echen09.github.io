History of Memorial Park

Military Park is Founded

The founders of Newark set aside a six-acre stretch that formed a triangle between Broad Street, Park Place, and Rector Street. It was initially referrd to as the "Middle Commons." In the 1700's it was referred to as the "Training Grounds" used for military training. And finally, "Military Park" from 1869 onwards.

Statue of General Philip Kearny Erected in Military Park

The statue of the one-armed Civil War hero and Newark native was the first public sculpture placed in Newark. The statue was initially commissioned in 1873 by the State of New Jersey to be put on display in Statuary Hall located in Washington D.C.. However, the statue was found in the cellar of the State House in Trenton in 1879. Once found, a Civil War veteran committee arranged to move the statue to Newark. Ulysses S. Grant and George B. McClellan attended the dedication ceremony. Henry Kirke, also famous for the casting of the George Washington statue in Union Square casted the bronze statue.
